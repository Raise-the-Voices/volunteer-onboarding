# Lobby an official

Lobbying just means asking someone with power to act. You don't need a title or
a meeting to start — a clear, well-aimed message to the right office is real
lobbying, and you can do it today.

**Goal:** send one message to one official (or their office) about an issue or
case you care about.

Keep **the Five F's** in mind — be **Fast, Fair, Frank, Friendly, Firm**.

- [ ] Pick your issue or case — reuse [your brief](/warmup/pick-your-issue.md)
- [ ] Find the right official: your own representative, or one with leverage over the situation (a relevant committee member, a foreign ministry, an embassy)
- [ ] Find how to reach them — contact form, email, or public social account
- [ ] Draft a short message:
  - who you are (a constituent / a concerned person)
  - the issue or case in **two sentences**
  - **one clear ask** ("Please raise this with…", "Will your office look into…?")
- [ ] Share your draft in **#general** for a quick check before sending
- [ ] Send it, and note that you did in **#general**

**Done when:** your message is sent and logged.

> Remember **Forgiveness, not permission** — you don't need sign-off to contact
> your own representative. But on a sensitive or unreported case, check with
> your team first: see [From practice to real cases](/work/active-cases.md).
