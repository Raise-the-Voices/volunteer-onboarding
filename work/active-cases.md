By now you've set up a meeting, picked an issue, found allies, and drafted an
outreach message. Those are the exact skills real casework needs. This page is
the bridge from practising to helping for real — at your own pace.

## How we work real cases

Some of the people we advocate for are in the news. Many are not — and when no
one is reporting a case, no one is applying any pressure. Quietly and carefully
getting a case *seen* is a big part of what changes things.

Because these are real people and real families, we work them with care:

- **We raise cases as questions, not accusations** — see [Raise a case with someone](/outreach/raise-a-case.md).
- **We check facts before we share anything**, and we only share what's safe to share.
- **We follow what the family wants** — sometimes that means withholding a name or a photo.
- **No one works a hard case alone.** You'll be paired with someone.

## You don't have to be ready today

There's no rush to jump into the heaviest work. Build up little by little:

1. Finish the warm-up tasks — they're the real skills, just on easy mode.
2. Pick a campaign below that fits you, and do one small thing on it.
3. When you feel ready, tell your team lead you'd like to take on a live case.
