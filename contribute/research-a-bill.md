# Research a bill

Laws are levers. Knowing which bill touches your issue — what it says, who's
behind it, what it would change — turns "someone should do something" into a
specific ask you can bring to a specific office.

**Goal:** find one bill or law relevant to your issue and summarize it in a few lines.

- [ ] Pick your issue — **Disappeared**, **Gaza**, **Sudan**, or your own
- [ ] Search for a relevant bill or law (try `<issue> bill congress`, `<country> sanctions act`, or a legislature's official bill tracker)
- [ ] Note four things: the bill's **name/number**, **who sponsors it**, **what it would change**, and **where it is now** (proposed, in committee, passed?)
- [ ] Write a few plain sentences a non-expert could follow, with a link to the official text
- [ ] Share it in the volunteer Slack — and flag if it points to an official worth contacting

**Done when:** your short bill summary is shared with the team.

> This feeds straight into [Find influencers](/outreach/find-influencers.md) and [Lobby an official](/outreach/lobby-an-official.md) — a bill's sponsor or a committee member is exactly the kind of person who has leverage.
