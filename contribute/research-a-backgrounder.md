# Research a backgrounder

Step one of the Learning Cycle is **understand** — and the way we share
understanding is a short *backgrounder*: a few paragraphs that give the big
picture, so the next volunteer starts ahead of where you did.

**Goal:** write one short backgrounder on an issue you care about.

- [ ] Pick an issue — **Disappeared**, **Gaza**, **Sudan**, or one of your own
- [ ] Spend 30–45 minutes reading from credible sources; note the 3–4 you'd cite
- [ ] Write a few paragraphs, plainly, for someone who knows nothing yet: what's happening, who it's happening to, how it got here, and what's at stake
- [ ] Link your sources so others can check it and build on it
- [ ] Post it in the volunteer Slack (**#general** or a country/area channel)

**Done when:** your backgrounder is shared, with sources.

> Facts with sources beat opinions. This is **step 1 of the Learning Cycle** (see [Read the handbook](/start/read-the-handbook.md)), and a backgrounder is one of the things we make — you can reuse it in [Pick your issue](/warmup/pick-your-issue.md) and every outreach task after.
