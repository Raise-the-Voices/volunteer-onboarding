# Comment in a thread

A good comment can move a conversation forward — surface a fact, point to a
case, or ask the question everyone's stepping around. The trick is to add, not
attack.

**Goal:** leave one constructive comment that helps move an issue forward.

- [ ] Find a public thread about an issue you care about — **Disappeared**, **Gaza**, **Sudan**, or your own — on social media, under a news article, or on an ally's post
- [ ] Write one comment that adds something: a fact with a source, a link to a case, or a genuine question
- [ ] Frame it as a **question, not an accusation** — "Is it true that…?" gets read; "You're ignoring…" gets deleted (same rule as [Raise a case with someone](/outreach/raise-a-case.md))
- [ ] Post it, then share the link in the volunteer Slack so the team can back you up

**Done when:** your comment is posted and shared with the team.

> Keep the **Five F's** in mind — be **Fair** (accurate), **Friendly** (you want a conversation, not a fight), and **Firm**. On a sensitive or unreported case, check with your team before posting — see [From practice to real cases](/work/active-cases.md).
